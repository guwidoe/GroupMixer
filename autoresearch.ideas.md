# Autoresearch ideas

Archive of solver3 objective-quality ideas that have already been **materially tried**, plus the learnings and conclusions from those attempts.

The live queue of strongest **untried** ideas now lives in `autoresearch.ideas-to-try.md`.

## Rules
- This file is for ideas that have already been **tried**, not for the untried queue.
- Prefer ideas that align with the lane goal: **new move types, richer neighborhoods, new search memory, new perturbation/restart strategies, new metaheuristics, and enabling solver3 refactors**.
- Small SA parameter tweaks belong here only if they were actually tried or are clearly in service of a larger mechanism that was tried.
- Once an idea from `autoresearch.ideas-to-try.md` is materially tried, move it here immediately and record the learning/conclusion.
- If an idea is part of a larger mechanism that needs incubation, keep updating its entry here through the incubation window.
- Promising but still-untried ideas belong in `autoresearch.ideas-to-try.md`, not here.

## Move types / compound operators
- Clique-aware hotspot session repair burst: materially tried as a stagnation-triggered macro operator that picked the highest-pressure session and applied up to three exact-recomputed improving block transfers within that session, where a movable unit was either a singleton or an entire active clique to preserve clique integrity. This was meant to be a broader ALNS-style repair step than the old tiny two-group kernel while staying compatible with the current loop. In first form it was clearly not competitive (`101.7291`): transfer-balance worsened, large_gender/synthetic did not improve, and the operator remained too myopic even with exact recomputation. A later stronger repair variant widened the exact neighborhood from one group-pair to a small whole-session reassignment problem: it selected up to four movable non-clique people contributing attribute imbalance and exactly enumerated compact cross-group reassignment combinations before the memetic burst. That stronger direct-reassignment form was less catastrophic but still uncompetitive (`101.6310`) and did not fix the chronic transfer/large_gender/synthetic blockers. Retire these direct block-transfer / direct reassignment repair forms for now. If a repair burst is revisited later, give it richer destroy+repair structure or swap/cycle coverage rather than greedy exact block transfers or direct single-person reassignment alone.

## Neighborhood families
- Naive best-of-k sampled neighborhood within existing families: tried two variants (swap/transfer `k=3` and swap-only `k=2`). The broader version was too expensive; the cheaper version still catastrophically harmed `stretch_sailing_trip_demo_real`. Retire this exact family for now. If neighborhood enrichment is revisited later, make it structurally different: target specific conflict patterns, add diversity/randomization guards, or evaluate compound moves rather than greedily taking the locally best sampled preview.

## Metaheuristics / search memory / perturbation
- Conditional incumbent restart / restart-cycle search: three variants were tried. Midpoint restart helped sailing once, but the family consistently regressed the aggregate or hotpaths. Retire for now; revisit only if a future neighborhood specifically benefits from a perturb-and-reoptimize driver.
- Exact-undo tabu on swap/transfer: tried a lightweight recent-reversal ban (`tenure=8`). It improved some cases but regressed the aggregate and added overhead. Retire this simple form for now; if search memory is revisited later, make it more state-aware (e.g. target repeated group-pair churn or repeated person-pair-contact regressions rather than blanket reverse-move bans).
- Late Acceptance Hill Climbing (LAHC): tried as a pure non-SA driver with a 256-score history window. It badly destabilized transfer-attribute-balance and large_gender. Retire this pure form for now; if revisited later, it needs a much stronger guard against constraint-heavy regressions.
- Online UCB move-family selection: tried two variants (plain improvement-rate UCB and cost-aware UCB). The plain variant was only mildly worse, but the cost-aware follow-up catastrophically harmed sailing. Retire this simple family-selection bandit for now unless a future version can use a richer reward signal tied to best-score improvements or structural conflict reduction.
- Guarded record-to-record acceptance is now the most promising non-SA family. The winning variant accepts improving moves plus bounded uphill moves within a shrinking band above best-so-far, using a linear `2 -> 0` threshold over `max(iteration_fraction, time_fraction)`. A quadratic close was worse, so if incubating further, tune around the linear schedule rather than closing the gate faster.
- GRASP-style multi-start outer driver: tried a naive equal-slice version that spent each fixed-time case across several diverse constructor seeds and polished each with the incumbent inner search, keeping the best schedule globally. This did produce real basin-diversity effects (social_golfer, sailing, and synthetic all improved), but it materially hurt large_gender and transfer balance and nearly doubled raw hotpath cost. A later front-loaded elite-scout variant also failed badly on the new machine: even with honest time accounting and fail-soft skipping of invalid constructor seeds, it still catastrophically collapsed sailing and regressed the aggregate. Retire these light constructor-portfolio forms for now. If this family is revisited later, it needs a much stronger constructor or a genuinely richer population/compatibility policy so extra starts earn their budget instead of fragmenting it.
- Freedom-aware GRASP-style constructor feeding the kept local search: first materially tried on the new machine as a solver3-local session rebuild pass after baseline construction. It rebuilt every session with a randomized restricted-candidate greedy heuristic that respected cliques/immovables and scored candidate groups using repeat/contact deltas plus attribute-balance and light pair-constraint heuristics before handing the schedule to the kept record-to-record search. The first result (`101.1285`) was not good enough to beat the current memetic keep, but it was directionally interesting: synthetic collapsed from a chronic weak spot to essentially reference (`1.0002`), sailing improved, and social_golfer stayed near reference, while transfer-balance and large_gender remained the main blockers. A balance-focused follow-up that rebuilt only the top attribute-pressure sessions with a stronger balance-biased greedy/RCL pass was materially worse (`101.6319`): it improved sailing and synthetic further, but large_gender regressed too much. This family is still not fully retired, but the obvious balance-focused follow-up did not solve the blocker. If revisiting later, use a different balance signal or more selective partial rebuild rather than simply pushing the constructor harder toward attribute-balance on hotspot sessions.
- ALNS-style session ruin-and-recreate: tried a whole-session rebuild operator with a fail-soft fallback. On prolonged stagnation it picked a high-pressure session and rebuilt it with a repeat-aware greedy repair while preserving immovables and clique integrity. This was a real large-neighborhood step and did not crash once made fail-soft, but the naive repair was not strong enough: it landed around `100.11` on the old machine and still trailed the then-kept record-to-record baseline. Retire this simple whole-session form for now; if ALNS is revisited later, use more selective destroy targeting and a much stronger repair heuristic.
- Targeted restricted repair kernel over a two-group subproblem: tried as an occasional stagnation-triggered exact repartition over one session/group-pair neighborhood, with immovables fixed and full oracle rescoring of each candidate repartition. On the new machine this was benchmark-honest but essentially inert: the aggregate stayed baseline-like (`101.35 -> 101.38`), indicating the trigger/coverage was too weak to matter. Do not retire the broader ALNS/LNS family yet, but retire this tiny two-group form as underpowered. If revisiting ALNS, use a larger hotspot destroy set, stronger repair coverage, or a more targeted hotspot selector.
- Memetic / crossover macro move: incubated first as constructor-seeded donor session crossover. The fail-soft one-session splice reached `99.37`, and the two-session splice reached `99.62` on the old machine. That made this family materially stronger than naive multi-start and the simple ALNS session-rebuild family, but it still trailed the then-kept record-to-record baseline. On the new machine, the first stronger variant was a one-shot stagnation-triggered offspring burst that briefly polished a diversified donor, picked the donor session with the best local repeat-pressure improvement, transplanted it into the incumbent, and resumed the main search within the same wall-clock budget. That first run was mildly positive (`101.3513 -> 101.2810`), but a confirmation rerun regressed (`101.4529`), which showed the local repeat-pressure selector was too noisy. A stronger follow-up then replaced that proxy with exact offspring rescoring across all allowed one-session donor transplants and kept the best offspring under the existing record-to-record gate; that version improved further to `101.0558`, and a confirmation rerun at `101.1196` stayed directionally positive versus the old baselines. A two-donor follow-up that split the same donor budget across two diversified donors and chose the globally best exact-rescored one-session offspring hit a stronger best result at `100.8462`, but its first confirmation rerun regressed sharply to `101.6589`, so that donor-choice extension remains promising-but-noisy rather than settled. Additional simple stabilizers have not held up: ranking donors by the average of their top two exact-rescored session-transplant outcomes failed (`101.2241`), and a short post-crossover session-local polish also failed (`101.1237`) despite helping social_golfer. A naive greedy extension that allowed a second donor-session transplant in the same burst was materially worse (`101.6311`), especially on transfer-balance, so retire that simple two-session stacking form. Two population-lite elite-archive follow-ups also failed to turn this into a real survivor-managed population: a strict within-run archive was effectively inert (`101.3645`), while a more permissive snapshotting/slack version became active but still uncompetitive (`101.2664`). A deeper survivor-managed donor-pool variant with multiple bursts likewise stayed stuck on essentially the same outcome (`101.2665`), so simple archive persistence and pool survivor rules are not enough. The first richer recombination-unit follow-up — exact evaluation of all one-session and two-session donor bundles from the same donor — also failed (`101.2944`) and slowed the hotpath, so the bottleneck does not seem to be merely greedy two-session interaction misspecification. Two donor-generation follow-ups also failed to unlock the family: incumbent-derived hotspot donor generation landed at `101.2650`, and a sharper donor-advantage selector regressed further to `101.3868`, which suggests naive session-pressure heuristics are too myopic for choosing what to import into a donor. A later cross-donor mixed-session recombination variant, which exactly rescored two-session offspring combining one session from donor A with one from donor B, was directionally better than the same-donor two-session bundle (`101.2038`) but still not competitive with the keep. A deeper recombine-then-polish child donor built from top session candidates of both donors regressed badly (`101.9771`), which suggests the family is not missing just one more layer of local optimization after session mixing. Finally, a benchmark-honest scout-then-intensify donor portfolio — briefly polishing four scout donors, ranking them by exact best-offspring potential, then intensifying the top two — was even worse (`102.2405`), so donor-budget fragmentation inside the burst is also a dead end. These results suggest passive pool reuse, myopic donor-generation tweaks, modestly richer session-bundle scoring, and donor portfolio splitting do not fix donor quality or compatibility. This family is still the most alive large mechanism in the lane because `903743d` remains the best keep, but the obvious local extensions are plateauing. If continuing, prefer a genuinely different donor-generation mechanism or a different macro neighborhood rather than more archive-threshold tweaks, naive pool management, donor-pressure heuristics, richer session-bundle rescoring, or scout-style donor-budget fragmentation.
- Structural tabu over session/group-pair churn: tried as a coarse group-pair/session memory with aspiration only for moves beating best-so-far. It was much worse than exact-undo tabu: sailing collapsed, social_golfer regressed, and representative/path hotpaths exploded. Retire this coarse form too. If search memory is revisited later, it should likely be finer-grained and used only inside a broader macro mechanism (e.g. post-repair or offspring intensification), not as a blanket corridor ban.
- Hypergraph / structural-compatibility enabling layer: first materially tried on the new machine as a lightweight session-level compatibility filter inside the memetic donor burst. It scored donor sessions by structural improvement (repeat-pressure plus attribute-balance pressure, with a shape-distance penalty) and only exactly rescored the top structurally promising transplants. This was benchmark-honest but too weak in first form (`101.3924`): it likely over-pruned useful donor-session options and did not stabilize large_gender or synthetic. Move this family out of the untried queue for now. If revisiting later, use richer structural signatures or compatibility features than a simple session-pressure-plus-shape-distance filter.

## Solver3 architecture / refactors enabling research
- Capability-aware benchmark-suite splitting for advanced-mode routing was materially tried in the new long-budget metaheuristic lane. The first experiment routed repeat-encounter stretch cases (`social_golfer`, `kirkman`, `large_gender`) through `single_state + sgp_week_pair_tabu` while keeping feature-rich stretch cases (`sailing`, `synthetic partial attendance`) on baseline truthful modes. Result: net regression (`100.0 -> 100.20445`). Social Golfer improved (`5409 -> 5367`), Kirkman stayed flat (`55`), but `large_gender` regressed (`2102 -> 2157`). A narrower follow-up kept the same idea but limited the SGP-local improver to the pure zero-repeat cases only (`social_golfer`, `kirkman`) and returned `99.9137`, i.e. a real aggregate improvement. Conclusion: a `RepeatEncounter` constraint alone is not enough to justify broad capability-routing into the SGP-local improver, but a **pure zero-repeat** routing rule is promising and worth incubating further.

## Benchmark / correctness observations worth following up on
- Champion stability on the new machine is materially noisy. The kept best memetic result remains `100.8462`, but two unchanged reruns of the same champion commit landed much worse (`101.3785`, `101.4063`). One rerun also showed notably slower raw runtime (`solver3_raw_score_us ~90.9` vs ~79.5 at the keep), which is consistent with some system-state drift; the second rerun recovered raw speed but not quality. Treat the current noise/stability band as wider than the early ~0.3 estimate until more same-code reruns are gathered.
